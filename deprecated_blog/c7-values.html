Exploring My Values

by Santa Maria, Steven * August 21, 2015

On the heels of my last blog post on stereotyping and how an "autopilot" took over me for many years, I thought I'd do a meta-analysis on my personal values. Why? Because sometimes life passes us by and we're no happier now compared to square one.

I gathered a list of values that I feel linked me to my happiest, proudest, or most satisfied times in my life. I also rated them 1-5 according to how I live up to these values (5 is more agreement):

  • Accomplishment - 2
  • Creativity - 2
  • Friendships - 5
  • Reputation - 4
  • Autonomy - 3
  • Privacy - 4
  • Community - 5
  • Intellectual status - 4
  • Knowledge - 4
  • Music - 2
  • Nature - 3
  • Volunteering - 5
  • Economic security - 2
  • Patriotism - 4
  • Self-respect - 4

A lengthy list indeed but the value I do want to highlight is "Self-respect." I feel that when you have respect for yourself first and foremost, it will lead to a whole host of other values like confidence and friendships. Self-respect is also the fuel for improving myself. With that said, recognizing this value and others will help me mediate those episodes of the stereotype threat or when I'm feeling defeated.
