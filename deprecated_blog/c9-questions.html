Right Questions for the Right Answer

by Santa Maria, Steven * September 6, 2015

As I dive deeper into my development career, I'm starting to realize the value and importance of the online community to get things done. What I mean by this is that I probably would have been stuck on a coding challenge if it hadn't been for others who asked questions on Quora or Stack Overflow and had them answered.

We can't however pose a question willy-nilly. There's a considered methodology of sorts that can make our questions more understandable and the answers we receive more helpful. Here are some tips I compiled that can help us with this:

  • In the question header, describe the problem concisely
  • Introduce the problem before any code
  • Post only the most relevant part of your code
  • Employ tags (of posts) to help describe and archive your question
  • Proof-read
  • Respond to feedback

With these tips in mind, we can help make the development community more productive not just for everybody else but for yourself too!
